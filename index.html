Are Langauge Models Overscaled? Slimming LLMs as Better Tool Learning Agents Details

Under the guidance of Professor Zhaohan Xi, Binghamton University, State University of New York


We investigates tool learning, posing key questions about the necessity of large-scale parameters and the optimal model scale for maintaining robust tool learning while minimizing resource consumption; our findings suggest that smaller LLM variants can perform comparably to larger ones in tool learning, leading to the creation of SLM, an open framework that integrates compression methods like pruning, quantization, and distillation to enhance tool learning capabilities across various scenarios, all while emphasizing the importance of developing efficient "LLM-as-tool-learning-agent" models without diminishing the value of larger foundation models.

Backchannel Understanding of Large Language Models Details

Under the guidance of Professor Yang Xu, SUSTech, Shen Zhen, Guang Dong, China



Backchannel in digital conversations permits private communication visible only to the sender and receiver. Backchannel is multithreaded, substantial, and governed by many social conventions; it persists only if captured in users' private logs. To better understand the backchannel's function—and to predict ways backchannel may be affected by application design and attempts to capture it on a server-wide scale for research and analysis—we analyzed private transcripts of conversation recorded in Map Task.

Predicting Wordle Result Based on Random Forest Details (Feb. 2023)

Contest: COMAP-MCM-C 2023 report
Wordle is a word guessing game that appears on the back of the New York Times newspaper and also has an online version for people to enjoy. The words are all five letters long, and players have a limited number of guesses. Each correct guess returns information on whether the guessed word is in the target word and if so, its correct position. We aim to predict the daily number of players for the company (using an ARIMA model), determine the distribution of the number of attempts for any given word (using Random Forests), and further assess the difficulty of guessing a particular word (using K-means clustering).

Momemtum: is it truly exist in tennis game? Details (Feb. 2024)

Contest: COMAP-MCM-C 2024 report
In the realm of sports, the concept of "momentum" is widely discussed among athletes. It's often described a player's perceived streak of success or failure, seemingly independent of their inherent skill level. For instance, a tennis player may experience strong performance one week but struggle the next, with factors like weather, mood, external conditions being blamed for the variance.
Our paper delves into the elusive nature of momentum in tennis.
