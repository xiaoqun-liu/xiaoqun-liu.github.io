BaR: A one-shot Block-aware Reconstruction Method after Post-training Pruning for Large Language Models

Yupeng Su, Ziyi Guan, Xiaoqun Liu, Tianlai Jin, Dongkuan Wu, Graziano Chesi, Ngai Wong, Hao Yu


As the scale of large language models (LLMs) continues to grow, the demand for efficient model compression and pruning techniques becomes increasingly critical. However, current approaches either require extensive retraining or involve post-training quantization and pruning, both of which are computationally expensive and consume substantial resources. To trackle this issue, we present BaR (Block-aware Reconstruction after Post-Training Pruning), a new and effective approach to prune LLMs.

Unlike traditional methods that primarily focus on single-layer sparsity, BaR integrates a comprehensive reconstruction process that prioritizes the recovery of regions with high sensitivity after initial sparsity, allowing further pruning of less sensitive areas. This method leverages a metric that multiplies weights by gradients, employing a Taylor series expansion to assess changes. Our findings indicate that first-order derivative information becomes more pronounced after the model undergoes a reconstruction process post-initial sparsity, providing a more accurate assessment for further pruning.
