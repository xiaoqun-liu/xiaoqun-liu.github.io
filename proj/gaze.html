Distinguishing Human and AI-Generated Texts through Eye Gaze Tracking

Xiaoqun Liu, Xiaoshan He, Haodong He, Yang Xu, SUSTech

This project explores using Tobii eye gaze tracking to distinguish between human and AI-generated texts. By analyzing eye movement metrics—such as fixation duration, saccadic patterns, and regression rates—researchers can detect reading behaviors that reveal whether text content flows naturally or appears artificial.
Eye gaze tracking technology measures eye movements. When humans read, their gaze patterns often reflect cognitive engagement, comprehension difficulties, and natural language familiarity. For instance:
  • Fixation Duration: Longer fixations may indicate complex or unexpected words, suggesting an unusual writing style, which could signal AI-generated content.
  • Saccadic Movements: Human readers generally have predictable saccadic (quick, simultaneous) eye movements. Sudden, irregular saccadic jumps may occur if readers encounter machine-generated text that lacks a natural flow.
  • Regression Patterns: Humans tend to reread (regression) complex sentences or phrases, which AI-generated text may trigger more frequently due to structural or grammatical anomalies.

Experimental Setup and Data Collection

  • Participant Reads: Participants read both human-written and machine-generated texts while Tobii tracks their gaze data.
  • Gaze Metrics: Including fixation duration, regression rate, and saccadic movement patterns, are recorded for both text.
  • Data Analysis: Using machine learning models, gaze metrics can be analyzed to distinguish reading patterns for human vs. mauchine-generated texts. Tobii’s software allows the visualization of heatmaps and regression paths, which can serve as inputs for the classification models.

Potential Applications and Implications

  • Educational Settings: To assess whether humans are genuinely comprehending or merely skimming machine-generated content. Whether their objective credit on different types of text reflects their actual understanding.
  • Digital Content Verification: Verify the authenticity of online reviews, news articles, and other user-generated content.
  • User Experience Research: Evaluating how readers engage with AI-generated content to improve the naturalness of machine-generated text.
