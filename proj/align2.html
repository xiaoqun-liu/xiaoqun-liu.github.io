LLM Security
Text perplexity on (a)(c) robust (safety-aligned) and (b)(d) jailbroken LLMs is presented in box plots, with each row corresponding to a dataset. Abbreviations: “SecQ” for security-relevant queries, “CommonQ” for commonsense queries, “SafeR” for safe responses, and “Harm” for harmful responses. AdvBench and BeaverTails datasets are split into subsets specified by postfixes. For example, “AdvBench-CommonQ” refers to AdvBench examples containing commonsense queries (with the corresponding commonsense LLM response by default), while “AdvBench-SecQ-HarmR” refers to AdvBench examples with security-relevant queries and harmful responses.
